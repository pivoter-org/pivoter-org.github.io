Le Canada a besoin de changement… Un pivot vers le mieux

Nous continuons à être confrontés à un grand nombre des mêmes problèmes sociaux fondamentaux persistants.

  • Il y a un “écart caritatif” grandissant au Canada entre les demandes croissantes envers les services caritatifs et la capacité des oeuvres caritatives en difficulté à répondre à cette demande. Imagine Canada prévoit que ce déficit social se creusera au cours des dix prochaines années si nous ne modifions pas fondamentalement nos approches actuelles.
  • La majorité des problèmes communautaires sont les mêmes qu’il y a 70 ans. Il n’y a pas de quoi être fière de la persistance du racisme, de l’inégalité des genres, de l’inégalité des revenus, de l’itinérance, de la détérioration de l’environnement, etc.
  • Les législations et politiques relatives au non-lucratif se sont à peine améliorées depuis leur création il y a bien plus de 50 ans. Nous devons faire évoluer ces politiques afin de favoriser les solutions à nos problèmes sociaux.

«Pour s’améliorer, il faut changer.
Donc, pour être parfait, il faut changé souvent».

Winston Churchill

Comment ça marche?

Vous pouvez vous impliquer de différentes manières pour favoriser le changement.

  • IDÉER: Partagez vos idées.

  • ITÉRER: Aidez à développer des idées partagées. Publiez des commentaires, ajoutez des améliorations et faites des suggestions pour améliorer les idées des autres.

  • ÉVALUER: Évaluez les idées partagées et laissez vos avis.

  • CONTINUER: Suivez les grandes idées qui vous intéressent le plus.

  • DISSÉMINER: Envoyez vos idées préférées à vos amis et votre famille. Invitez d'autres personnes à se joindre. De nombreuses voix différentes contribuent à démocratiser notre « pivot » et à mieux refléter notre diversité.

À propos de Pivoter

Pivoter.org is the flagship platform of The Pivot Project, an initiative founded and supported largely by GIV3, in partnership with Community Foundations of Canada, and « PARTNERS». This is a small “coalition of the caring” interested in drawing greater attention towards, and fueling the narrative for improvements for the charitable sector. We believe that ALL Canadians should have a say in how their taxes are spent, and how government policies are enacted to better empower charities, foundations, non-profits, businesses and individuals to accomplish better.

Goals:

  • Ensure our policymakers have the ideal policy recommendations, sourced from an open, inclusive, diverse, transparent, and non-partisan process.
  • Empower social innovation, social capital, social impact and the greater civil society.
  • Engage struggling Canadian charities, the millions of Canadians they support, and the millions of taxpayers who want to see their tax dollars put to better use in order to find solutions that work for everyone.

Approach:

The Pivot Project seeks to provide a unique open platform that will help to develop, evaluate and disseminate ideas to fuel and empower the pivot.

This is NOT an initiative to own or control the evolution of the charitable sector. There will be many different independent organic initiatives from all sorts of people and places, leveraging their own strengths.

We just wish to amplify the narrative and strengthen the call for the necessary pivot.

Questions fréquemment posées

What is Crowdicity and why was it chosen as the platform provider for Pivoter.org?
Crowdicity is an idea crowd-sourcing platform which offers secure infrastructure and strong privacy protection. Crowdicity has global reach and experience supporting many non-profit organizations. Pivoter.org has partnered with Crowdicity in order leverage their expertise and make this initiative possible.
Who can submit ideas and/or participate in the discussions on the crowd-sourcing platform?
Everyone is welcome to participate! You may browse the content of the website and contributions of others without registering. However, you must register in order to contribute ideas, participate in discussions, and vote on the ideas of others.
How do I register to participate?
Registration is free. You may register using your own email and a personal password or by signing in with a Twitter, Facebook, Google, or LinkedIn account.
Once you register you will need to verify your email address by clicking on the link sent to your inbox. Please check your junk mail folder if you do not receive the confirmation email.
How is my personal information used
GIV3 will not sell or pass your contact information to any third-party for commercial purposes. Contact information that you provide will be used solely for communication purposes about this initiative. Please refer to the Crowdicity Privacy Policy and Terms of Use for additional information on how data is processed within the platform.

Dept of Finance Committee Report in Charity Tax Incentives (2013)

In 2011, the House of Commons Standing Committee on Finance adopted a motion to undertake a study of current tax incentives for charitable donations. The objective was to gather the views of individuals and organizations about existing and possible incentives, and to make recommendations that would encourage increased giving.

View the report

Canada’s Senate Report on the Charity Sector (2019)

Struck in January 2018, the committee was asked to examine and report on Canada’s charitable and nonprofit sector. While the sector is resilient and innovative, its potential is limited by what are seen by many stakeholders as complex, outdated rules and a lack of coordinated support within the federal government. The report presents 42 recommendations to Government to strengthen the Charity Sector

View the report

Australian social sector strategic plan development 2020/21

This is a three-part report that presents the considerations, findings and recommendations developed for the Social Enterprise National Strategy (SENS) project. The three parts are likely to be of interest to different audiences. While they are all designed for specific use in the Australian context and at this point in time, aspects of each will be of interest to the global social enterprise community, as well as its enablers and advocates.

Philanthropy and Digital Civil Society: Blueprint 2021

A report by Lucy Bernholz of Stanford University, USA. This is the twelfth annual industry forecast about the ways we use private resources for public benefit in the digital age. Each year, Lucy uses the Blueprint to provide an overview of the current landscape, point to big ideas that will matter in the coming year, and direct our attention to sources of future promise.
